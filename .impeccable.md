## Design Context

### Users

**Citizens / Residents** (Barangays 251, 252, 256 — Tondo, Manila)
- Filing incident reports on mobile, often in stressful or urgent situations
- May not be tech-savvy; some may be elderly or using low-end Android devices
- Job to be done: report a problem quickly and feel confident it was received and will be acted on
- Emotional need: **reassurance** — "the barangay has this, I can trust the system"

**Barangay Officials**
- Reviewing, triaging, and updating incident statuses throughout the workday
- Power users who live in the dashboard — need efficient scanning, not handholding
- Job to be done: manage their queue, make decisions, coordinate response
- Emotional need: **control and clarity** — a clean, spacious view they can act from without noise

**Super Admins**
- Cross-barangay oversight, user management, audit logs, analytics
- Least frequent users per feature; need confidence in data integrity
- Emotional need: **authority and oversight** — the system is accountable and legible

---

### Brand Personality

**3 words**: Trustworthy · Clear · Civic

**Voice**: Calm, direct, institutional but never cold. Like a competent public servant — reassuring without being casual, professional without being intimidating.

**Emotional goals**:
- Citizens feel *held*, not interrogated
- Officials feel *in control*, not overwhelmed
- The system feels *accountable* — accurate, precise, logged

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Primary reference**: Notion / Linear — clean, purposeful, designed for people who work in it daily. No decoration for decoration's sake. Every element earns its place.

**Anti-reference**: Government website circa 2010 — no harsh borders, table-heavy layouts, outdated form fields, low-contrast text, or bureaucratic visual weight.

**Official / Admin views**: Clean government portal aesthetic — spacious, structured, calm. Easy to scan at a glance. Data-forward without being dense. The Notion/Linear standard applied to civic infrastructure.

**Citizen views**: Safe and reassuring. Simple, warm, frictionless. The navy/white palette communicates institutional trust; the step-by-step form flow reduces anxiety. No urgency aesthetics in the filing flow.

**Theme**: Light mode primary. Dark mode is supported but secondary. Never default to dark-mode-with-glowing-accents.

**Existing tokens (respect these)**:
- Primary Navy: `#00236f` / `#1e3a8a` — authority, trust
- Ochre Gold: `#865300` / `#feb14c` — analytics, secondary actions
- Critical Red: `#b91c1c` — urgent incidents, errors only
- Success Green: `#059669` — resolved, positive
- Fonts: Public Sans (headlines), IBM Plex Sans (body/UI), IBM Plex Mono (IDs/data)
- Border radius: `0.5rem` — approachable, not pill-shaped
- Shadows: subtle ambient only; no harsh drop shadows
- No 1px solid borders for layout sections; prefer color/background shifts

---

### Design Principles

1. **Reassuring authority over alarming urgency** — The interface should make citizens feel the system is capable and calm, not panicked. Reserve red and urgency signals strictly for critical status, never decoration.

2. **Purposeful density, not clutter** — Officials scan queues all day. Information should be dense enough to be efficient, but never overwhelming. Use whitespace and hierarchy to guide the eye, not fill space.

3. **Civic clarity** — Every word, label, and status should be unambiguous. Microcopy should sound like a competent public servant, not a SaaS tooltip. No jargon, no redundancy.

4. **Role-appropriate simplicity** — Citizens get stripped-back, one-thing-at-a-time flows. Officials get the full picture. The same underlying data, designed twice for two different contexts.

5. **Modern without novelty** — TUGON should feel like it was designed in 2025, not 2010 — but also not like a startup demo. No gradients for impact, no glassmorphism for effect, no animations that aren't functional. The Notion/Linear standard: calm confidence, not flash.
